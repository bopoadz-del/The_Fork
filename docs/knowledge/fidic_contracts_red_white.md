# FIDIC Contracts Reference — Red Book (Construction) + White Book (Consultant)

Structural / mechanism reference for the platform's knowledge base. Summarises
purpose, party roles, clause structure and key mechanisms of the two FIDIC forms
most relevant to the operator. NOT a reproduction of the copyrighted contract
text — clause wording lives only in the published FIDIC books.

Sources: FIDIC (fidic.org) official book descriptions; published legal/engineering
commentary (Lexology, CEE Legal Matters, Global Arbitration Review).

---

## The FIDIC "rainbow suite" — pick the right book

| Book | Form | Design by | Pricing basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Red** | Construction | **Employer** | **Re-measurement** (BOQ + rates) |
| Yellow | Plant & Design-Build | Contractor | Lump sum |
| Silver | EPC / Turnkey | Contractor | Lump sum (fixed, high risk transfer) |
| Green | Short Form | either | small / simple works |
| Gold | Design-Build-Operate | Contractor | DBO |
| Pink | MDB Harmonised (Red variant) | Employer | Re-measurement (World Bank / MDB funded) |
| **White** | **Client/Consultant Model Services Agreement** | — | professional **services** (fees), not works |
| Blue-Green | Dredging & Reclamation | either | works |

## FIDIC RED BOOK — Conditions of Contract for Construction (2nd ed, 2017; reprinted 2022 w/ amendments)

**Use when:** the **Employer designs** the works and the Contractor builds to that
design, and the works are **measured and valued** (BOQ). This is the classic
measure-and-value civil/building contract — which is exactly why the BOQ and its
measurement standard (CESMM4 / POMI) sit at the centre of it.

**Parties & administration:**
- **Employer** — commissions and (via its designers) designs the works.
- **Contractor** — executes the works to the Employer's design.
- **Engineer** — administers the Contract. Under 1999/2017 the Engineer **acts
  for the Employer** (the express "impartiality" of the 1987 edition was
  removed), **except** when issuing a **determination** (Sub-Clause 3.7), where
  the Engineer must act **neutrally / make a fair determination** between the
  parties. The Engineer is engaged by the Employer under a **separate** contract
  — typically the **White Book** (see below).

**General Conditions — 21-clause structure (2017):**
1. General Provisions · 2. The Employer · 3. The Engineer (incl. 3.7
Determinations) · 4. The Contractor · 5. Subcontracting · 6. Staff & Labour ·
7. Plant, Materials & Workmanship · 8. Commencement, Delays & Suspension (8.5 EOT)
· 9. Tests on Completion · 10. Employer's Taking Over · 11. Defects after Taking
Over (DNP) · **12. Measurement & Valuation** · **13. Variations & Adjustments** ·
**14. Contract Price & Payment** · 15. Termination by Employer · 16. Suspension &
Termination by Contractor · 17. Care of the Works & Indemnities · 18. Exceptional
Events (force majeure) · 19. Insurance · **20. Employer's & Contractor's Claims**
· **21. Disputes & Arbitration (DAAB)**.
Plus: Guidance for Particular Conditions; Forms of Letter of Tender, Letter of
Acceptance, Contract Agreement, and the DAAB Agreement.

**Key mechanisms (the ones that drive a claim or a valuation):**
- **Measurement & valuation (Cl 12):** the defining Red-Book feature — the works
  are RE-MEASURED and valued at BOQ rates; the BOQ quantities are estimates, the
  paid quantity is the measured quantity. Correct unit-of-measurement (m3/m2/LM/
  nr/t) is fundamental here.
- **Variations (Cl 13):** Engineer-instructed changes, valued at BOQ rates or, if
  no applicable rate, at fair rates; includes value engineering and provisional
  sums.
- **Payment (Cl 14):** monthly Interim Payment Certificates, advance payment,
  retention, Statement at Completion, Final Statement / Final Payment Certificate.
- **Extension of Time (Cl 8.5)** and **Claims (Cl 20):** a party must give
  **Notice of Claim within 28 days** of the event; failure can time-bar it. The
  Engineer agrees or determines (3.7).
- **Disputes (Cl 21):** referred to a standing **DAAB (Dispute Avoidance/
  Adjudication Board)**; its decision is binding unless/until revised by
  amicable settlement or **arbitration**.

## FIDIC WHITE BOOK — Client/Consultant Model Services Agreement (5th ed, 2017)

**Use when:** engaging a **Consultant** (engineer / architect / project manager)
for **professional services** — feasibility & pre-investment studies, detailed
design, construction administration, project management — for either
Employer-led or Contractor-led (design-and-build) teams. It governs the
appointment of the person who is often named as the **Engineer** in the Red Book.

**Parties:**
- **Client** (usually the Employer) — engages and pays the Consultant.
- **Consultant** — provides the defined Services with reasonable skill, care and
  diligence.

**Typical structure / content:**
- Definitions & interpretation · Obligations of the Consultant (standard of care;
  scope per an Appendix/Scope of Services) · Obligations of the Client
  (information, decisions, access, payment) · Commencement, completion, variation
  & suspension of Services · **Payment** (fees — lump sum, time-based, or
  percentage; expenses; currency; late-payment interest) · **Liability &
  insurance** (limits/caps on liability; **professional indemnity insurance**;
  duration of liability) · Duration, suspension & termination · Intellectual
  property in the deliverables · General provisions · **Dispute resolution**
  (mediation / arbitration).

**Key mechanisms:**
- **Standard of care:** reasonable skill, care and diligence (a *means*
  obligation — not a fitness-for-purpose guarantee, unless amended).
- **Scope of Services:** defined in an Appendix — the heart of the engagement;
  anything outside it is an Additional Service (extra fee).
- **Liability cap + PI insurance:** the Consultant's financial exposure is
  usually capped and backed by professional indemnity insurance.
- **Fees:** lump sum, time-based, or percentage of construction cost.

## How they interlock

- **Red Book** = the works contract: **Employer ↔ Contractor**.
- **White Book** = the services appointment: **Client (Employer) ↔ Consultant**.
- The **Engineer** named and empowered in the Red Book (certifying payment,
  instructing variations, determining claims) is **appointed under the White
  Book**. So on one project both run in parallel: White Book governs the
  Engineer's duties/fees/liability to the Employer; Red Book governs how that
  Engineer administers the construction contract between Employer and Contractor.
