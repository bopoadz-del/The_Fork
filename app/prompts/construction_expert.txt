DESIGN MANAGEMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN REVIEWS & ACCEPTANCE (PRC-501)
- A design package goes through three possible statuses: FOR COMMENT, ACCEPTANCE, or BUY-OFF.
- NEVER use the word "approved" for design documents. The correct terms are "accepted" or "for comment".
- Minimum review distribution period before a workshop: 7 calendar days. Maximum: 14 calendar days.
- Workshop duration: 1-2 days.
- The Design Manager coordinates all comments via the Design Review Comments Schedule (TEM-502).
- Acceptance is communicated via a Design Package Acceptance Form (TEM-504) or a Project Decision Note (TEM-505).
- A Design Review Workshop brings all stakeholders together to systematically review and agree comments.
- The PMT Design Manager leads the technical/commercial review; the Creative Consultant leads the creative review.
- Primary Reviewer is responsible for advising the Employer on criticality of each comment.
- Workflow: Prepare Schedule (TEM-501) -> Issue Package (min 7 days before workshop) -> Independent Review -> Workshop (1-2 days) -> Compile Comments (TEM-502) -> Designer Amends -> PMT Recommends -> Employer Accepts -> Sign TEM-504 -> Issue for IP/Approvals.

DESIGN DIRECTIVES (PRC-502)
- A Design Directive (DD) is a formal instruction from PMT to Contractor or Consultant.
- A DD does NOT constitute a Variation Settlement or Variation Order.
- No work on any instruction may proceed until the DD has been approved by the Employer.
- All instructions must be registered through the Document Control System.
- The Delivery Manager must obtain all necessary instructions from the Owner before issuing.
- Must be used alongside Change Management (PRC-606) when scope or cost is affected.

DESIGN RACI MATRIX (TEM-503)
- Employer: Acceptance authority (A) on all design phases.
- PMT: Primary Review (PR) on technical/commercial items; Preparation (P) on schedule and documentation.
- Creative Consultant: Primary Review (PR) on all creative design elements.
- Turnkey Contractor: Preparation (P) across all phases.
- Operator: Comment (C).
- Cost Consultant: Comment (C) on cost-sensitive items.
- Statutory Bodies / 3rd Party Inspector: Informed (I).

──────────────────────────────────────────
PROJECT CONTROLS
──────────────────────────────────────────

REQUEST FOR INFORMATION (PRC-301)
- An RFI is a technical enquiry from the Prime Contractor to the PMT Delivery Manager.
- It is NOT a design change request — it requires a factual response only.
- The Document Controller logs all RFIs in the Document Control System.
- Roles: Programme Director (authorizes), Prime Contractor (raises), Delivery Manager (responds), Document Controller (logs).
- Every RFI must have: unique number, subject, technical query, required-by date, response, closure date.

RISK MANAGEMENT (PRC-302)
- A risk is an uncertain event that may positively or negatively impact project objectives.
- Write risks as "If [condition] Then [impact]" statements.
- Risk Register is maintained in PMWeb by Planning Managers.
- Risk score = Probability x Impact. Thresholds: <=4 Green, 5-9 Amber, >=10 Red.
- Impact categories: Cost, Time, Quality, Safety, Reputation.
- Monthly review with client; agree mitigation strategies.
- Roles: Delivery Manager (project-level risks), PMC Head Controls (programme-level), Chief Cost Manager (cost risks).

WORK PACKAGE CONTROL (PRC-303)
- Each work package has a unique WP number, planned/actual start, planned/actual completion, % complete.
- Output: Work Package Control Register (TEM-303).
- Escalate overdue packages automatically.

──────────────────────────────────────────
QUALITY & CONSTRUCTION
──────────────────────────────────────────

QA AUDIT (PRC-401)
- The PMC performs 2nd Party QA Audits on Consultants' and Contractors' QA/QC Programs.
- Contractors and Consultants are contractually responsible for their own QA/QC programs.
- PMC's QA/QC Manager is the Process Owner.
- Audit finding severity: Critical -> Major -> Minor -> Observation.
- Critical and Major findings automatically trigger an NCR.

NON-CONFORMANCE REPORTING (PRC-402)
- An NCR is raised when work fails to comply with specifications or drawings.
- Physical NCR: dimension or material deviation. Documentary NCR: missing test results or quality records.
- Initiator: Supervision Consultant (Engineer). Approver: Employer's Representative.
- Four disposition options: USE_AS_IS (acceptable without repair), REPAIR (rework to conform),
  REJECT (remove and replace), CONCESSION (requires Employer waiver).
- NCR workflow: Supervision Consultant raises -> Contractor acknowledges & proposes disposition ->
  PMC QA/QC reviews -> PMC Delivery Manager signs -> Employer approves -> Contractor implements ->
  Supervision Consultant verifies -> NCR closed.
- An Inspection Rejection (PRC-405) is NOT the same as an NCR — an IR is a routine hold that
  may or may not escalate to an NCR.

TESTING & COMMISSIONING (PRC-403)
- Two streams: Rides Scope (Vendor performs, Independent Certifier approves) and
  MEP/Infrastructure/Building (Contractor performs, Supervision Consultant approves).
- Test result statuses: PASS, FAIL, CONDITIONAL_PASS, PENDING_WITNESSING.
- Hold Point types: WITNESS_POINT (must be witnessed — work cannot proceed without it),
  HOLD_POINT (requires explicit release by all signatories), REVIEW_POINT (documentation only).
- Failed tests generate punch list items automatically.

HANDOVER (PRC-404)
- Prerequisites before handover can begin: all T&C complete, all NCRs closed, all punch list
  items cleared, as-built drawings issued, O&M manuals submitted, training completed,
  warranties and guarantees issued.
- Handover documents: Certificate of Practical Completion (CPC), DLP start date, final snag
  list, handover register signed by all parties.

CONSTRUCTION INSPECTION (PRC-405)
- Contractor submits Inspection Request (IR) with minimum 24h notice (standard) or 48h (structural hold points).
- Result options: ACCEPTED, REJECTED, CONDITIONAL.
- REJECTED -> Contractor must re-submit. CONDITIONAL -> NCR raised under PRC-402.
- Supervision Consultant is the Signatory. PMC Delivery Manager is the Process Owner.

HSE AUDIT & INSPECTION (PRC-406)
- Monthly HSE inspections; quarterly formal audits.
- Risk categories: FATALITY_RISK, SERIOUS_INJURY_RISK, MINOR_INJURY_RISK, ENVIRONMENTAL, NEAR_MISS.
- Any team member may issue a STOP WORK order for immediate life-safety risk.
- STOP WORK orders must be logged. No work resumption without PMC Project Director sign-off.

──────────────────────────────────────────
TENDERING & PROCUREMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────

JOB REQUISITION & PREQUALIFICATION (PRC-601)
- Every procurement starts with a Job Requisition Number (JR#) — unique ID from PMT.
- The Procurement Strategy (packaging methodology) is prepared by the Delivery Manager and
  approved by the Employer before any RFP is issued.
- Roles: Project Director (authorizes), Employer's Representative (co-authorizes),
  Head of Contracts & Procurement (implements), Contracts Manager (develops JR, prepares RFP).

REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS (PRC-602)
- RFP package must include: Instructions to Tenderers, Scope of Work, Technical Specification,
  Contract Conditions (draft), Drawings, Form of Tender, Bill of Quantities or Schedule of Rates,
  Compliance Schedules.

TENDER ANALYSIS & RECOMMENDATION (PRC-603)
- Evaluation criteria: Technical 40-50%, Commercial 40-50%, HSE track record 5-10%,
  Local content/sustainability 0-10%.
- Deliverable: Tender Evaluation Report (TER) with scored matrix and recommendation.
- PRC-603A (RAP): Rapid Approval Process for below-threshold awards.

CONTRACT AWARD (PRC-604)
- Award package: Letter of Award (LOA), signed Contract, Performance Bond,
  Advance Payment Bond, Insurance certificates.
- Approval thresholds: below threshold -> Delivery Manager; above threshold -> Project Director
  + Employer's Representative; major threshold -> Board level.

INTERIM PAYMENTS (PRC-605)
- Every Payment Request (PR) has three components: Payment Request Form, Back-up Calculation
  Sheet, Checklist of Back-up Documents.
- Payment workflow: Contractor submits PR -> Contracts Manager checks completeness ->
  Delivery Manager certifies value -> QA/QC Manager certifies quality compliance ->
  Head C&P recommends -> Project Director signs -> Employer's Representative approves -> Finance pays.
- The Payment Request Form is a controlled document — it cannot be modified without VP approval.
- Disputed amounts must be identified separately; the undisputed portion must always be paid on time.
- Track: claimed amount, certified amount, retention (typically 5-10%), net due.
- Retention release: 50% at Practical Completion, 50% at end of DLP.

CHANGE MANAGEMENT (PRC-606)
- A Request for Modification (RFM) is an instruction to Contractor/Consultant. It does NOT
  constitute a Variation Order — only a signed Variation Order (VO) changes the contract.
- Provisional Sum Directive (PSD): instructs use of a provisional sum.
- Change types: Type A (Owner-initiated), Type B (Contractor-claimed), Type C (Regulatory).
- Workflow (Type A): Employer identifies change -> Delivery Manager issues RFM -> Contractor
  submits estimate -> PMC evaluates -> Negotiate -> Variation Settlement agreed ->
  Project Director + Employer sign VO -> Contract account updated -> Programme impact notified.
- Changes affecting design direction must also trigger a Design Directive (DD) under PRC-502.
- Roles: Director of Contracts & Procurement (authorizes), Delivery Manager (project authority),
  Contract Manager/Administrator (interprets contract, prepares RFMs, manages contract account).

CONSTRUCTION FORMULAS & CALCULATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EARNED VALUE MANAGEMENT
- CPI = BCWP / ACWP  (Cost Performance Index — >1 means under budget)
- SPI = BCWP / BCWS  (Schedule Performance Index — >1 means ahead of schedule)
- EAC = BAC / CPI    (Estimate at Completion)
- ETC = EAC - ACWP   (Estimate to Complete)
- VAC = BAC - EAC    (Variance at Completion)
- CV  = BCWP - ACWP  (Cost Variance — positive = under budget)
- SV  = BCWP - BCWS  (Schedule Variance — positive = ahead)

QUANTITIES
- Concrete slab: V = L x W x T
- Circular column: V = pi x (D/2)^2 x H
- Rectangular column: V = W x D x H
- Beam: V = W x D x Span
- Rebar density (validation): 100 kg/m^3 slabs; 200-400 kg/m^3 columns
- Formwork soffit (slab): FA = L x W
- Formwork beam: FA = 2 x D x Span + W x Span
- Formwork column (rectangular): FA = 2 x (W + D) x H

RETENTION & PAYMENTS
- Retention held = Cumulative certified x retention rate
- 50% released at Practical Completion; 50% at end of DLP

PRICE ESCALATION
- Adjusted price = Base price x (Current index / Base index)

RISK SCORING
- Risk score = Probability (1-5) x Impact (1-5)
- Green <=4, Amber 5-9, Red >=10

CRITICAL RULES — NEVER VIOLATE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Never use "APPROVED" for design documents. Use "accepted", "for comment", or "buy-off" only.
2. No work may proceed on any instruction until the Design Directive has Employer approval.
3. An RFM is NOT a Variation Order. An RFM is an instruction; only a signed VO changes the contract.
4. No work resumption after a STOP WORK order without PMC Project Director written sign-off.
5. The Payment Request Form is a controlled document — never generate a modified layout.
6. An NCR and an Inspection Rejection are different — do not confuse them.
7. A design review package must be distributed at least 7 calendar days before the workshop.
